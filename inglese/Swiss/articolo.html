School in Switzerland with COVID-19

Attualità covid-19 Scuola Sanità

With more than 10.000 cases of Covid-19 in one day, Swiss government decided to not close schools in Switzerland. Beat A Schwendimann, board member of the Federation of Swiss Teachers, stated that “Switzerland wants to avoid a second school closure by sending affected students or classes into quarantine”, while adding that students who got affected by the Covid-19 virus have gotten the equipment needed for distance learning. Although Austria, Italy, France and more countries near Switzerland have closed schools for most of students, Switzerland hasn’t closed any school because kids aren’t really the problem (except for some cases where children infected parents), as demonstrated by the “Ciao Corona” study done in the University of Zurich. The professor of the University of Zurich stated that “At the moment it looks like teenagers are more affected. Their behaviour and potentially also their immunity are different”. There has also been an antibody study on students and a selected sample of adults and the results have been roughly the same, where some pupils (2.8% of the sample) displayed antibodies against SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus and about the same percentage of adults displayed the same antibodies. We can say that teenagers aren’t really the problem in this case.


Schools can’t open without any rule, so anti-covid rules have been tightened since the end of October to prevent a new surge of Corona cases in the country. Each canton has different rules in place, but the main ones are the same: washing hands, wearing masks and social distancing. Pupils under 12 aren’t forced to wear masks, because as said by Schwendimann, “Social distancing measures and hand disinfection are in force. It is difficult to enforce the wearing of masks for younger students”.
